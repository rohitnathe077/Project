Organisation of Indian Army

Command Structure

The Indian Army is organized into the following commands:

  • Army Headquarters - Located in New Delhi, headed by the Chief of Army Staff (COAS)
  • Operational Commands - Six operational commands each headed by a Lieutenant General:
    • Northern Command (Udhampur)
    • Western Command (Chandimandir)
    • Central Command (Lucknow)
    • Eastern Command (Kolkata)
    • Southern Command (Pune)
    • South Western Command (Jaipur)

Combat Arms

The Indian Army's combat arms include:

  • Infantry
  • Armoured Corps
  • Artillery
  • Air Defence Artillery
  • Army Aviation Corps
  • Corps of Engineers
  • Corps of Signals

Field Formations

The Indian Army's field formations are organized as:

  • Corps - Commanded by Lieutenant General
  • Division - Commanded by Major General
  • Brigade - Commanded by Brigadier
  • Battalion - Basic combat unit commanded by Colonel

Support Services

Various support services ensure the army's operational effectiveness:

  • Army Service Corps (ASC)
  • Army Medical Corps (AMC)
  • Army Ordnance Corps (AOC)
  • Corps of Electronics and Mechanical Engineers (EME)
  • Military Intelligence Corps
  • Military Police Corps
