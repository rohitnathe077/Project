    Important Personnel

    General Manoj Pande
    (Chief of the Indian Army )

    General Manoj Pande, superannuated today after more than four decades of distinguished service and relinquished the appointment of the Chief of the Army Staff (COAS). His tenure will be remembered for high state of combat readiness, impetus to the process of transformation besides his strong push towards Atmanirbharta initiatives.

    General Upendra Dwivedi
    (Chief of Defence Staff (CDS))

    General Upendra Dwivedi, PVSM AVSM ADC (born 1 July 1964) is a serving four star general officer of the Indian Army. He is the current and 30th Chief of the Army Staff. He took over as the 30th COAS succeeding General Manoj Pande on 30 June 2024.     Indian Army Forces

    MARCOS

    MARCOS (Marine Commandos), is a special forces unit that was raised by the Indian Navy in 1987 for direct action, special reconnaissance, amphibious warfare and counter-terrorism.The MARCOS are capable of undertaking operations in all types of terrain but are specialised in maritime operations. The force has undertaken numerous joint exercises with special forces from around the world. As of 2012, the MARCOS has about 2,000 personnel though the exact number remains classified.

    Para Commandos

    Formed in 1966, the Para Commandos are part of the highly-trained Parachute Regiment of the Indian Army and are the largest part of the Special Forces of India. History:- The Para Commandos were formed in 1966 after the Indo-Pakistani war of 1965. The 50th Parachute Brigade was created in 1941 during World War II, which is the origin of the Para Commandos' heritage.The Para Commandos are an elite special forces unit that specializes in a variety of roles, including counter-terrorism, counter-insurgency, hostage rescue, direct action, and special reconnaissance.

    Ghatak Force

    True to its name ghatak. Every infantry battalion in the Indian Army has one platoon and only the most physically fit and motivated soldiers make it to the Ghatak Platoon. Ghatak commandos are shock troops who spearhead assaults ahead of the battalion. They are known for their hand-to-hand combat skills. Ghatak Platoons (Also referred to as Ghatak Force) are special operations capable reconnaissance platoons consisting of Shock troopers these are present in every infantry battalion of the Indian Army.

    COBRA

    COBRA (Commando Battalion for Resolute Action) is a specialised unit of the CRPF that was formed to counter Naxalism in India.

    Force One

    Force One came into being in 2010 after the 26/11 terrorist strikes in Mumbai. The prime role of this special elite force is to protect Mumbai from terrorist attacks.

    Special Frontier Force

    Raised in the aftermath of the 1962 Sino-Indian war as a special force for covert operations behind Chinese lines in the event of another war with China.

    National Security Guard

    The National Security Guard is India's premier counter-terrorist force. The NSG provides security to VIPs and is responsible for neutralising terrorist threats to vital installations.

    Garud Commando Force

    Formed in 2004, the Garud Commando Force is the special forces unit of the Indian Air Force. The training for being a Garud is the longest among all Indian Special Forces.

    Special Protection Group

    The Special Protection Group is a security force of the Government of India that is responsible for the protection of the Prime Minister of India and former Prime Ministers.

    More about Force One

    Force One is a special operations unit of the Mumbai Police, formed in response to the 26/11 attacks. It is trained to handle high-risk situations and counter-terrorism operations, ensuring the safety and security of the city.

    Equipped with advanced weaponry and technology, Force One is tasked with protecting Mumbai from future threats and maintaining law and order.
